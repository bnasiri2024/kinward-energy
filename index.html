Why The Seat Exists

Failure is decided in development — long before construction.

Engineers design what they're asked to design. Lawyers paper what they're told to paper. Capital advisors model the project as it's described to them. None of them is responsible for whether the whole thing is coherent.

A failed interconnection study can cost $5M and eighteen months. A misread grid upgrade can swing project economics by tens of millions. By the time these surface at FID, they're 10× more expensive to fix.

Geography
