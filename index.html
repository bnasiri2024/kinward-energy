The Infrastructure Problem

Most infrastructure projects fail in development — before a shovel hits the ground.

Developers commit capital to projects built on assumptions that don't survive contact with reality — grid constraints that weren't modeled, technology configurations that can't be financed, utility timelines that destroy returns.

The result isn't a construction problem. It's a development problem. And it compounds at every stage: bad assumptions become bad designs, bad designs become unbuildable projects, unbuildable projects become stranded capital.

"The gap between what developers assume and what utilities, engineers, and capital markets will actually support has never been wider — or more expensive."
⚡

Grid Access Failures

Interconnection queue positions lost, substation capacity assumptions wrong, transmission upgrade costs that destroy project economics.

🏗️

Constructability Gaps

Technology configurations that look good on paper but can't be built on schedule, on budget, or to the performance specs required for financing.

💰

Capital Structure Misalignment

Debt and equity assumptions that don't match the actual risk profile of the project — discovered at financial close, not development entry.

🔌

Technology Integration Failures

On-site generation, storage, and grid systems that weren't designed to work together — or with the utility interconnection requirements.
